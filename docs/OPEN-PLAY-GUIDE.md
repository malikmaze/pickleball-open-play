# How SisClub Open Play works

A short guide for players. Share this link or copy the sections below for your group chat.

---

## Joining a session

1. Open the **Schedule** tab and pick a session.
2. Tap **Join** and enter your name, contact number, gender, and **skill level**.
3. No account or password needed — your details are saved on your phone for next time.

If the session is full, you join the **waitlist** automatically. You’ll be added when a spot opens.

---

## Skill levels — pick honestly

| Level | Who it's for |
|-------|----------------|
| **Newbie** | First time playing pickleball (or any racket sport). Still learning to hit the ball. |
| **Beginner** | Some pickleball or racket sport experience. Can rally a bit. |
| **Novice** | Know the rules and can keep a short rally going. |
| **Intermediate Low** | Consistent play, working on strategy. |
| **Intermediate High** | Strong rallies, competitive open play. |
| **Advanced** | Very experienced or tournament-level. |

**First time?** Choose **Newbie**. We use this to group you with other beginners when possible.

---

## Before you play

1. **Join** the session online before you arrive (or ask the organizer to add you).
2. If payment is required, follow the session’s payment instructions and wait for confirmation.
3. When you arrive, **check in with the organizer**. You’re not in the playing queue until you’re checked in.

You can **cancel online** anytime before check-in (while your status is still “Joined” or “Payment confirmed”). After check-in, ask the organizer if you need to leave.

---

## The queue — who plays next?

Once you’re checked in, you enter the **queue**. Courts are filled in **fair order**:

1. **Fewest games played** — if you haven’t played yet, you’re ahead of someone who already played.
2. **Longest wait** — among players with the same number of games, whoever’s been waiting longer goes first.

You can see your **queue position** and **live courts** on the **Courts** tab or on your session status page.

---

## Newbie-friendly matching

If you signed up as **Newbie**:

- When **4 or more Newbies** are waiting, the next open court is filled with **Newbies only**.
- If there are only **1–3 Newbies** waiting, you may play with more experienced players until more Newbies arrive. The organizer can also hold a court if they want a full beginner group.

This helps first-timers learn without always facing advanced players.

---

## Partners

Want to play doubles with a friend?

- Link up as **partners** with the organizer (Queue tab at the session).
- **Mutual partners stay together** — you won’t be split onto different courts.
- Partners are placed **next to each other** in the queue so you’re called at the same time.

---

## On court

- **4 players** per court — two teams of two.
- Teams are balanced by skill when possible.
- Games use the session’s **target score** and **win by** rules (shown on the live page).
- When your game ends, you go back to the queue for the next round.

---

## Watching live

Use the **Courts** tab to:

- See who’s on each court
- Follow the queue and who’s **next up**
- View recent winners and activity

No need to join — anyone can watch.

---

## Quick tips

- **Be on time** — check in when you arrive so you don’t lose your place.
- **Pick the right skill level** — especially Newbie if it’s your first time.
- **Keep your phone handy** — check queue position and court assignments on the app.
- **Questions?** Talk to the organizer on site.
